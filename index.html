UI Design &
Disinformation

This project is the result of Miguel Gutierrez's year-long research fellowship with theInternet Research Initiative
at UCLA (2018-2019).  Under mentorship from The Knight Foundation's Lilian Coral, he studied the effects to
which elements of user interfaces (UIs) have on the distribution of disinformation online. 

What is Disinformation?

Disinformation is false information intended to mislead; Internet subcultures take advantage of the current
media ecosystem to manipulate news frames, set agendas, propagate ideas, “troll,” profit, and instigate discord.

Disinformation of the social media variety has been the subject of intense academic debate.
However, the current literature around disinformation centralizes on two main camps of inquiry:
disinformation as content or disinformation in the context of datafication and algorithms.

I want to think about the disinformation phenomenon from a new angle.

Scholarly work has analyzed disinformation primarily on the journalistic or technical (i.e. algorithmic) aspects, but are there not prospects to the distribution of disniformation inherent of the platform disinformation agencies are utilizing?

The user interface is a factor of digital experiences that is seldom mentioned in disinformation discourses. As a product designer, I recognize the power design has in shaping human behavior, and there is a vast body of research supporting this. Why aren't we talking more about interface design?   

Here's my hypothesis...

The UI design of social media platforms, particularly with respect to information architectures (IA) and their given affordances, directly affects the distribution of user-generated content. Considering the advent of disinformation online, platforms allocating users greater control of the delivery and distribution of networked information have a better line of defense against disinformation agencies.

Methodology: how this project works

1

UI Theory

I will first demonstrate the theoretical underpinnings of how design operates and why design is a crucial consideration for communication systems and society at large. I will introduce terms necessary for understanding the dimensions design has in digital space.   

2

Case Study

Here, I will be looking at Facebook specifically as a point of reference. I will explore Facebook's philosophy towards the relationship between design, disinformation, and people, but I will also investigate how/if this relationship is realized in Facebook's users through empirical user research. 

3

Application

Considering both the theoretical implications of UI design and identifying Facebook's shortcomings identified through the case study, how might we improve UI design to resist disinformation? The 'Application' section will propose design solutions to the issue of disinformation.
